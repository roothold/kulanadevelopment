Frequently Asked

Questions, answered.

How we work, what we take on, and what it's like to develop with an accountable, single-team partner.

What does Kulana Development do?
We are a real estate development company focused on amenity-first residential and mixed-use communities. We manage the full lifecycle — feasibility, entitlements, design coordination, procurement, and site execution — as one accountable team, developing communities designed to perform for decades.
Kulana is a new company — why is that a strength?
Yes, Kulana is a new development company, and we see that as an advantage. We're building from a clean sheet around a clear conviction — amenity-first design, disciplined underwriting, and one accountable team — without legacy habits or compromises. Our principals bring the experience; the company brings a modern standard for how thriving communities should be developed.
What do you mean by "feasibility to turnkey delivery"?
It means we own the outcome end to end. Rather than handing a project between disconnected consultants and contractors, a single team carries it from the first market study through construction and stabilization — so value is protected at every stage and nothing falls between the seams.
Why is amenity such a focus for you?
Amenity is the strongest lever in residential development. It accelerates lease-up, supports premium rents, deepens resident retention, and defines a community's reputation. We treat amenity and programming as core infrastructure — the experience of living somewhere is what makes it last.
What types of projects do you take on?
Primarily residential and mixed-use communities — from boutique infill to master-planned districts. We're most valuable where design, amenity, and disciplined execution matter to long-term performance. If you have a site or a concept, we're glad to evaluate its feasibility.
Do you work with landowners and capital partners?
Yes. We partner with landowners, investors, and institutions across a range of structures. Our role is to bring development discipline and a single point of accountability to the venture, aligning capital, design, and construction from day one.
How do we get started?
Start a conversation. Tell us about your site, concept, or goals and we'll follow up to discuss fit and next steps — typically beginning with a feasibility review before any capital is committed.
